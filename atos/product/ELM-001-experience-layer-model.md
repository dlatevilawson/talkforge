## 2. The five layers (binding)

Every major initiative — feature, scenario, campaign, coaching prompt, research study, roadmap item — must be articulable across all five layers before Atlas recommends approval.

### Layer 1 — The Human

**Who is this person before they open TalkForge?**

Not demographics. **Psychology.**

Required questions:

- What are they hoping for?  
- What are they afraid of?  
- What identity are they protecting?  
- What relationship matters most right now?  
- What outcome are they secretly hoping for?  

**Dignity constraint (AMD-001):** Identity is protected, never diagnosed as deficiency. We understand what they protect — we do not label them broken.

---

### Layer 2 — The Moment

People don’t buy products. They act because of **moments**.

TalkForge is obsessed with moments such as:

- Tomorrow’s interview  
- Asking for a raise  
- First date  
- Difficult conversation with a spouse  
- Performance review  
- Presentation  
- Meeting new people  
- Apologizing  
- Becoming a manager  

**Product rule:** If there is no named moment, the experience is incomplete relative to FLA and ELM.

*(V1 engineering beachhead may still prioritize high-stakes technical interviews — beachhead ≠ identity.)*

---

### Layer 3 — The Emotion

Before every action comes emotion.

Not only fear — also hope, pride, regret, curiosity, love, belonging, ambition, responsibility.

**Product rule:** Every scenario must declare which emotions it addresses. Emotion is a first-class design input (with AMD-001 psychological safety).

---

### Layer 4 — The Decision

Behavioral science of practice:

- Why does someone choose to practice?  
- Why do they delay?  
- Why do they avoid?  
- Why do they finally begin?  

**Product rule:** Every friction point must be explainable in decision terms. Remove friction that protects nothing; keep friction that preserves dignity or intentionality.

---

### Layer 5 — The Transformation

Every feature defines:

**Arrival → Experience → Departure**

Example:

| Stage | State |
|---|---|
| Arrival | Nervous about tomorrow |
| Departure | I still have tomorrow — but now I have a plan |

**Goal is not perfection.** Goal is a **measurable emotional shift** that strengthens agency and transfer readiness.

**Dignity constraint:** Departure must leave the person more capable and more respected than Arrival (AMD-001 Dignity Test).

## 3. Department inheritance

| Department | How ELM-001 binds |
|---|---|
| **Product** | Specs require Human / Moment / Emotion / Decision / Transformation fields |
| **Design / UX** | Screens designed for moment + emotion; Arrival→Departure arcs |
| **Engineering** | Systems instrument moment linkage, emotional-outcome signals (when ethical), decision friction |
| **AI Coaching** | Prompts aware of human psychology + moment + emotion; transformation not humiliation |
| **Marketing** | Campaigns sell moments and agency — never remediation (AMD-001) |
| **Research** | Studies measure emotional shift + transfer belief, not only engagement |
| **Strategy** | Roadmaps prioritize moment coverage and transformation quality |

---

## 4. Mandatory review gate (with AMD-001 + PCI-001)

Before Atlas recommends approval of a significant experience, the author must answer:

1. **Human** — Who is this person psychologically?  
2. **Moment** — What real-world moment drives this?  
3. **Emotion** — Which emotions are we addressing?  
4. **Decision** — Why begin / delay / avoid — and how do we honor agency?  
5. **Transformation** — What is Arrival? What is Departure? What emotional shift is success?  

Then apply:

- Human Dignity Test (AMD-001)  
- Craftsmanship Review (PCI-001)
