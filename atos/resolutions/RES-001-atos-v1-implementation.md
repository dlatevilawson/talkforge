## Purpose

This Resolution formally defines the implementation scope, authority, priorities, and success criteria for the initial implementation of the Atlas TalkForge Operating System (ATOS).

Its purpose is to transition ATOS from an approved governance architecture into an operational organizational system while preserving constitutional stability and implementation discipline.

This Resolution serves as the official authorization to begin ATOS Version 1.0 implementation.

## Background

The following architectural phases have been completed:

- ATOS Specifications (Level 1) — designed
- ATOS Standards (Level 2) — designed
- ATOS Operating Manuals (Level 3) — designed (outlines)
- ATOS Reference Architecture (Level 4) — designed (catalog)
- Repository Discovery
- Repository Gap Analysis
- Architecture Review Board (ARB)

## Resolution

The Founder approves the implementation of ATOS Version 1.0 according to the governance established by the approved architecture.

Implementation shall prioritize organizational integrity, governance, maintainability, and long-term scalability over implementation speed.

No implementation activity may violate the approved governance hierarchy.

## Out of Scope

- Future executive departments not currently required
- Advanced autonomous AI capabilities beyond approved executive roles
- Large-scale product architecture redesign unrelated to governance
- Experimental governance models
- Unapproved constitutional amendments
- Fully autonomous organizational decision-making without Founder oversight

## Minimum Viable Implementation (MVI)

ATOS Version 1.0 is successfully implemented when:

**Governance:** All six Specifications exist with approved authority; core Standards implemented; authority relationships enforced.

**Knowledge:** One canonical knowledge authority; one governed promotion pipeline; Draft cannot become Canonical without approval.

**Operations:** One operational source of truth; duplicate operational records eliminated; operational registries functioning.

**Repository:** Structure reflects ATOS; required metadata present; ownership assigned; version control established.

**Runtime:** Interfaces for Hub, Context Injector, Memory Keeper, and Sandbox defined; workflows follow approved governance.

**Executive Systems:** Atlas and Sentinel operate under approved charters; authority and responsibilities documented; interactions follow governance.

## Implementation Principles

1. Governance before implementation.
2. Identity before execution.
3. Documentation before automation.
4. One canonical source of truth.
5. Traceability over convenience.
6. Long-term maintainability over short-term optimization.
7. Executive accountability for every organizational artifact.
8. No duplication of authority.
9. Every document has an owner.
10. Every decision is recorded.

## Founder Authority

The Founder retains exclusive authority over constitutional amendments, Specification/Standard approval, organizational restructuring, executive appointments, major architectural changes, and version releases.

No AI Executive may assume these responsibilities.

## Delegated Authority

**Atlas may:** coordinate implementation, recommend priorities, organize work, monitor progress, produce executive reports.

**Sentinel may:** review engineering, protect architecture, validate implementations, investigate technical issues.

Neither executive may alter constitutional governance.

## Declaration

Upon Founder approval, this Resolution authorizes the implementation of ATOS Version 1.0.

All implementation activities shall proceed under this Resolution until superseded by a future approved resolution.
