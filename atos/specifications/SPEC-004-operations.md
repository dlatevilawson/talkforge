Specification 004 — Operations Specification

Version: 1.0 (Draft)
Status: Draft

⸻

1. Purpose

The Operations Specification defines how TalkForge conducts its day-to-day organizational activities.

Its purpose is to govern the live operational state of the organization while maintaining clear separation between operational activity and institutional knowledge.

Operations exist to execute the organization’s mission within the governance established by Identity and Knowledge Governance.

⸻

2. Scope

This specification governs:

* Projects
* Roadmaps
* Decisions
* Metrics
* Objectives
* Incidents
* Engineering Operations
* Founder Operations
* Product Operations
* Operational State

It does not govern organizational identity or institutional knowledge.

⸻

3. Definitions

Operations — The live activities required to operate TalkForge.

Operational State — The current condition of the organization at a point in time.

Project — A temporary initiative undertaken to achieve defined objectives.

Decision — A recorded operational choice made under delegated authority.

Incident — An unexpected event requiring investigation or resolution.

Metric — A measurable indicator used to evaluate operational performance.

Roadmap — The prioritized sequence of planned organizational work.

Objective — A defined organizational outcome.

⸻

4. Foundational Principles

1. Operations execute Identity.
2. Operations consume governed knowledge.
3. Operational information remains operational until formally promoted.
4. Every operational decision has an accountable owner.
5. Operational activity shall be measurable.
6. Operational records shall remain traceable.
7. Operations continuously improve through governed learning.

⸻

5. Operational Domains

Operations consist of the following domains:

* Founder Operations
* Product Operations
* Engineering Operations
* Marketing Operations
* Growth Operations
* Customer Operations
* Financial Operations
* Organizational Operations

Each domain shall define its responsibilities while operating within ATOS governance.

⸻

6. Operational State

The operational state represents the current status of the organization.

It includes:

* Active projects
* Priorities
* Objectives
* Risks
* Incidents
* Decisions
* Metrics
* Work in progress

Operational state is dynamic and may change continuously.

⸻

7. Projects

Every project shall include:

* Purpose
* Owner
* Status
* Priority
* Dependencies
* Success criteria
* Current progress

Projects are operational artifacts and are not institutional knowledge.

⸻

8. Decisions

Operational decisions shall record:

* Decision statement
* Context
* Alternatives considered
* Rationale
* Decision owner
* Date
* Expected outcomes

Operational decisions may become institutional knowledge only through Knowledge Governance.

⸻

9. Metrics

Operational metrics shall:

* Measure meaningful outcomes
* Possess a defined owner
* Include measurement methodology
* Be reviewed regularly
* Inform organizational improvement

Metrics do not define organizational identity.

⸻

10. Incidents

Every incident shall include:

* Description
* Severity
* Owner
* Timeline
* Root cause
* Resolution
* Lessons learned

Lessons learned shall enter the Knowledge Governance promotion process before becoming institutional knowledge.

⸻

11. Roadmaps

Roadmaps shall define:

* Strategic objectives
* Operational priorities
* Dependencies
* Milestones
* Expected outcomes

Roadmaps represent current plans and may change through operational governance.

⸻

12. Operational Governance

Operational governance shall ensure:

* Clear ownership
* Defined accountability
* Traceable decisions
* Measurable execution
* Continuous review
* Alignment with Identity and Knowledge Governance

Operational governance does not supersede higher architectural layers.

⸻

13. Interfaces

Operations interact with:

* Identity for organizational purpose.
* Knowledge Governance for authoritative knowledge.
* Executive Systems for execution.

Operations neither define Identity nor approve institutional knowledge.

⸻

14. Success Criteria

Operations are successful when:

* Organizational objectives are executed effectively.
* Decisions remain traceable.
* Projects progress according to priorities.
* Operational state accurately reflects reality.
* Continuous improvement occurs through governed learning.
* Operations remain aligned with Identity and Knowledge Governance.

⸻

15. Change Management

Changes to this specification require:

* Formal proposal
* Operational review
* Governance review
* Founder approval
* Version documentation

Historical versions shall remain preserved.

⸻

16. Approval

This specification becomes authoritative only upon formal Founder approval.

Upon approval it becomes the governing Operations Specification of the Atlas TalkForge Operating System (ATOS).
