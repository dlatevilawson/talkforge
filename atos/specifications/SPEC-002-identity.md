Specification 002 — Identity Specification

Version: 1.0.0
Status: Authoritative

⸻

1. Purpose

The Identity Specification defines the permanent identity of TalkForge.

Its purpose is to establish the immutable principles that define who TalkForge is, why it exists, and the governing authority from which all organizational decisions derive legitimacy.

Identity is the highest architectural layer within ATOS.

It is independent of technology, products, organizational structure, and implementation.

Every subsequent specification, operational process, executive system, and organizational decision must remain consistent with the Identity Specification.

⸻

2. Scope

The Identity Specification governs:

* Constitution
* Founder Brief
* Forge Laws
* Organizational Philosophy
* Mission
* Vision
* Core Values

Identity does not govern operations, implementation, products, or technology.

⸻

3. Definitions

Identity — The permanent character of TalkForge.

Mission — The organization’s enduring purpose.

Vision — The future TalkForge seeks to create.

Constitution — The highest governing document defining authority and principles.

Founder Brief — The founder’s permanent intent for the organization.

Forge Laws — Organizational laws that guide decision-making.

Philosophy — The beliefs that shape behavior and culture.

⸻

4. Foundational Principles

1. Identity is permanent.
2. Mission precedes strategy.
3. Principles precede decisions.
4. Character precedes capability.
5. Every organizational action must align with Identity.
6. Identity may evolve only through formal governance.

⸻

5. Identity Components

Identity consists of:

* Constitution
* Founder Brief
* Forge Laws
* Organizational Philosophy
* Mission
* Vision
* Core Values

Each component has a unique responsibility and must not duplicate another.

⸻

6. Authority

Identity is the highest authority within ATOS.

No subordinate specification may modify, reinterpret, or contradict Identity.

⸻

7. Responsibilities

Identity is responsible for:

* Defining purpose
* Defining principles
* Defining organizational character
* Defining governing authority
* Providing organizational continuity

Identity is not responsible for operational execution.

⸻

8. Interfaces

Identity provides governing principles to:

* Knowledge Governance
* Operations
* Executive Systems

Identity consumes no lower-layer authority.

⸻

9. Governance

Changes to Identity require:

* Written proposal
* Supporting rationale
* Review against Constitution
* Founder approval
* Version documentation

Identity changes are exceptional events.

⸻

10. Success Criteria

Identity is successful when:

* Organizational decisions remain mission-aligned.
* Leadership acts consistently with principles.
* AI executives reason consistently with organizational values.
* Organizational continuity survives leadership, technology, and product changes.

⸻

11. Change Management

Every approved amendment must include:

* Version
* Date
* Summary
* Rationale
* Approving authority

Historical versions remain preserved.

⸻

12. Approval

This specification becomes authoritative only upon formal Founder approval.

Upon approval it becomes the governing Identity specification for TalkForge.
