Specification 003 — Knowledge Governance Specification (TKGS)

Version: 1.0 (Draft)
Status: Draft

⸻

1. Purpose

The Knowledge Governance Specification defines how TalkForge creates, evaluates, approves, maintains, consumes, and archives organizational knowledge.

Its purpose is to ensure that organizational knowledge remains accurate, governed, traceable, and consistent throughout the lifetime of TalkForge.

Knowledge Governance is the second architectural layer of ATOS and governs how the organization learns.

⸻

2. Scope

This specification governs:

* Knowledge Types
* Knowledge Lifecycle
* Evidence Standards
* Confidence Framework
* Canonical Sources
* Promotion Rules
* Version Governance
* Knowledge Integrity
* Knowledge Consumption
* Knowledge Archival

It does not govern organizational identity, operational execution, or executive responsibilities.

⸻

3. Definitions

Knowledge — Information recognized and managed under the rules of this specification.

Institutional Knowledge — Knowledge formally approved as authoritative.

Operational Knowledge — Live information used to operate the organization.

Observation — Unverified information awaiting evaluation.

Evidence — Information supporting or refuting a claim.

Canonical Knowledge — The approved source of authority for a knowledge domain.

Promotion — The governed process by which knowledge advances toward institutional authority.

Confidence Level — The organization’s assessed certainty regarding a knowledge artifact.

⸻

4. Foundational Principles

1. Knowledge is governed.
2. Evidence precedes authority.
3. Institutional knowledge is earned, not assumed.
4. Every knowledge artifact has a traceable origin.
5. Every knowledge domain has one canonical source.
6. Knowledge remains versioned and auditable.
7. Historical knowledge is preserved, not erased.

⸻

5. Knowledge Types

Knowledge shall be classified as:

* Identity Knowledge
* Institutional Knowledge
* Operational Knowledge
* Working Knowledge
* Observations
* Historical Knowledge

Each artifact shall belong to one knowledge type.

⸻

6. Knowledge Lifecycle

Knowledge progresses through the following lifecycle:

1. Observation
2. Working Knowledge
3. Investigation
4. Evidence Collection
5. Evidence Review
6. Recommendation
7. Executive Review
8. Institutional Approval
9. Canonical Knowledge
10. Historical Archive

Knowledge may not bypass lifecycle stages unless explicitly authorized by governance.

⸻

7. Evidence Standards

Every institutional claim shall include:

* Origin
* Supporting evidence
* Confidence level
* Review history
* Approving authority

Assertions without evidence shall not become institutional knowledge.

⸻

8. Confidence Framework

Every knowledge artifact shall possess one confidence level:

* Observation
* Hypothesis
* Investigated
* Verified
* Canonical
* Superseded
* Archived

Confidence levels communicate certainty, not authority.

⸻

9. Canonical Sources

Each knowledge domain shall have exactly one canonical source.

Duplicate sources of authority are prohibited.

Operational systems may maintain working copies but may not redefine canonical knowledge.

⸻

10. Promotion Governance

Promotion requires:

* Evidence
* Review
* Recommendation
* Approval
* Documentation
* Versioning

No operational information automatically becomes institutional knowledge.

⸻

11. Knowledge Integrity

Knowledge Governance shall continuously ensure:

* No conflicting canonical sources
* Traceable knowledge lineage
* Version consistency
* Integrity validation
* Preservation of historical records

Integrity violations shall be investigated before institutional approval.

⸻

12. Knowledge Consumption

Every human and AI system shall consume knowledge according to its authority and responsibilities.

Consumers may read institutional knowledge but may not alter it outside approved governance.

Operational knowledge shall be consumed according to organizational role and authorization.

⸻

13. Knowledge Archival

Superseded knowledge shall be archived.

Historical records shall remain:

* Traceable
* Searchable
* Versioned
* Immutable

Archived knowledge remains part of the institutional record.

⸻

14. Governance

Changes to this specification require:

* Formal proposal
* Evidence
* Architectural review
* Founder approval
* Version documentation

⸻

15. Success Criteria

Knowledge Governance is successful when:

* Institutional knowledge remains consistent.
* Organizational learning is governed.
* Duplicate authority is eliminated.
* Evidence supports institutional claims.
* Human and AI executives reason from the same authoritative knowledge.

⸻

16. Change Management

Every approved amendment shall include:

* Version
* Date
* Summary
* Rationale
* Approving authority

Previous versions shall remain preserved.

⸻

17. Approval

This specification becomes authoritative only upon formal Founder approval.

Upon approval it becomes the governing Knowledge Governance Specification of the Atlas TalkForge Operating System (ATOS).
