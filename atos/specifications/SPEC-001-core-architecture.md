ATOS Specification 001 — Core Architecture

Version: 1.0 

⸻

1. Purpose

The Atlas TalkForge Operating System (ATOS) is the governing operating system for TalkForge.

Its purpose is to provide a permanent architectural framework that defines how the organization establishes its identity, governs knowledge, operates its business, and executes work through humans and AI systems.

ATOS exists to ensure that TalkForge can grow without losing consistency, integrity, or institutional continuity.

It is technology-agnostic and is designed to outlast individual AI models, software implementations, and organizational changes.

Every system, process, executive agent, and future component of TalkForge must operate within the rules defined by ATOS.

Scope

ATOS governs the organizational architecture of TalkForge.

Its scope includes:

* Organizational identity
* Knowledge governance
* Operational governance
* Executive systems
* Human decision-making
* AI decision-making
* System interactions
* Organizational standards
* Governance processes

ATOS does not define implementation details, application code, infrastructure, programming languages, databases, or vendor-specific technologies.

Implementation exists to serve ATOS.

ATOS does not exist to serve implementation.

3. Definitions

For the purposes of ATOS, the following terms have the meanings defined below.

ATOS
The Atlas TalkForge Operating System that governs the organization.

Identity
The immutable principles that define who TalkForge is.

Knowledge Governance
The system that governs how knowledge is created, evaluated, approved, maintained, consumed, and archived.

Operations
The live organizational activities, projects, decisions, metrics, and workflows required to operate TalkForge.

Executive Systems
The human and AI executives responsible for carrying out organizational responsibilities within the governance defined by ATOS.

Institutional Knowledge
Knowledge that has completed the approved governance process and is recognized as authoritative by the organization.

Operational Knowledge
Current working information used to operate the organization that has not necessarily become institutional knowledge.

Canonical Source
The single authoritative source for a defined category of organizational knowledge.

Governance
The rules, responsibilities, and approval processes that ensure organizational consistency, integrity, and accountability.

4. Foundational Principles

ATOS is governed by the following foundational principles:

1. Identity precedes execution.
    Every decision and action must align with the organization’s defined identity.
2. Knowledge is governed.
    No information becomes institutional knowledge without an approved governance process.
3. Operations remain operational.
    Live operational information does not become institutional knowledge unless formally promoted.
4. Execution follows governance.
    Human and AI executives operate within the authority and constraints defined by ATOS.
5. One source of authority.
    Every governed domain must have one canonical source of truth.
6. Technology serves the organization.
    ATOS is independent of any specific AI model, software platform, programming language, or implementation.
7. Institutional continuity is preserved.
    Organizational knowledge, governance, and identity must remain consistent across technological, organizational, and leadership changes.

5. Architectural Layers

ATOS consists of four governing layers.

Layer 1 — Identity

Defines the immutable identity of TalkForge.

Includes:

* Constitution
* Founder Brief
* Forge Laws
* Philosophy

⸻

Layer 2 — Knowledge Governance

Defines how organizational knowledge is created, validated, approved, maintained, consumed, and archived.

⸻

Layer 3 — Operations

Defines the live operational state of the organization, including projects, decisions, metrics, incidents, roadmaps, and operational workflows.

⸻

Layer 4 — Executive Systems

Defines the human and AI executives responsible for executing organizational responsibilities within the governance established by the preceding layers.

Each layer depends upon the layers above it and must not violate their authority.

6. Layer Authority

Authority within ATOS flows from higher layers to lower layers.

The order of authority is:

1. Identity
2. Knowledge Governance
3. Operations
4. Executive Systems

No lower layer may override, contradict, or redefine a higher layer.

If a conflict exists between layers, the higher layer takes precedence.

Executive Systems execute organizational responsibilities but possess no authority to modify Identity, Knowledge Governance, or Operations except through their respective governance processes.

Authority within ATOS is hierarchical, while execution may be collaborative.

7. Responsibilities

Each architectural layer has a distinct responsibility.

Identity

Defines who TalkForge is and establishes the organization’s immutable principles, purpose, and governing authority.

Knowledge Governance

Defines how organizational knowledge is created, evaluated, approved, maintained, consumed, and archived.

Operations

Defines how TalkForge conducts its day-to-day activities, manages organizational state, and executes business processes.

Executive Systems

Execute organizational responsibilities, make decisions within their delegated authority, and operate in accordance with Identity, Knowledge Governance, and Operations.

Each layer is responsible only for its defined scope and must not assume responsibilities assigned to another layer.

8. Interfaces

The architectural layers interact only through defined interfaces.

* Identity provides governing principles to all lower layers.
* Knowledge Governance provides the rules for managing organizational knowledge.
* Operations provides the current operational state required for execution.
* Executive Systems consume governed knowledge and operational information to perform organizational responsibilities.

Information may flow between layers only through approved governance processes.

No layer may bypass the authority or responsibilities of another layer.

9. Governance

ATOS is the highest governing specification within TalkForge.

Any modification to ATOS must follow a formal governance process.

Changes to ATOS require:

* A documented proposal.
* Supporting evidence and rationale.
* Evaluation of alternatives and tradeoffs.
* Review for consistency with existing specifications.
* Formal approval by the designated governing authority.
* Versioned documentation of the approved change.

No component, policy, process, or executive system may establish authority that conflicts with ATOS.

ATOS remains the authoritative framework from which all subordinate specifications derive their authority.

10. Success Criteria

ATOS is considered successful when:

* Every organizational system operates within the architectural hierarchy defined by ATOS.
* Identity, Knowledge Governance, Operations, and Executive Systems remain clearly separated in responsibility and authority.
* Every organizational decision can be traced to the governing specifications that authorize it.
* Institutional knowledge remains consistent, governed, and free from conflicting sources of authority.
* Human leaders and AI systems operate from the same authoritative organizational framework.
* The operating system remains stable and adaptable as TalkForge grows in people, products, technology, and organizational complexity.

11. Change Management

ATOS is a controlled specification.

All approved changes must be recorded in a version history that includes:

* Version number
* Date of approval
* Summary of changes
* Rationale for the change
* Approving authority

No revision may modify or supersede an existing specification without documenting its impact on dependent specifications.

Superseded versions shall be retained as part of the organization’s historical record and must remain traceable for governance and audit purposes.
