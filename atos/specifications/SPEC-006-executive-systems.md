Specification 006 — Executive Systems Specification

Version: 1.0 (Draft)
Status: Draft

⸻

1. Purpose

The Executive Systems Specification defines the human and AI executives responsible for executing the mission of TalkForge.

Its purpose is to establish a governed executive organization that transforms Identity, Knowledge Governance, and Operations into effective execution while preserving organizational integrity, accountability, and long-term continuity.

Executive Systems execute organizational responsibilities but do not establish organizational authority.

⸻

2. Scope

This specification governs:

* Executive roles
* Executive authority
* Executive responsibilities
* Executive collaboration
* Executive decision-making
* Executive accountability
* Executive specialization
* Executive succession

It does not govern Identity, Knowledge Governance, Operations, or Runtime Infrastructure.

⸻

3. Definitions

Executive System — A human or AI executive responsible for organizational execution.

Executive Role — A defined organizational responsibility with delegated authority.

Delegated Authority — Authority granted through ATOS to perform defined responsibilities.

Executive Collaboration — Coordinated execution between multiple executive systems.

Executive Recommendation — A reasoned proposal requiring appropriate approval before becoming organizational policy.

⸻

4. Foundational Principles

1. Executive Systems execute but do not govern.
2. Authority is delegated, not assumed.
3. Every executive has a clearly defined responsibility.
4. Executive recommendations shall be evidence-based.
5. Executive collaboration shall strengthen organizational outcomes.
6. Executive decisions remain traceable.
7. Executive Systems remain accountable to ATOS.

⸻

5. Executive Organization

Executive Systems consist of human executives and specialized AI executives.

Initial executive systems include:

* Founder
* Atlas (Chief of Staff)
* Sentinel (Chief Engineering Officer)
* Product Executive
* Marketing Executive
* Growth Executive
* Customer Executive
* Finance Executive

Additional executive systems may be created through approved governance.

⸻

6. Executive Authority

Executive authority is delegated by ATOS.

Executive Systems may:

* Make recommendations
* Execute delegated responsibilities
* Coordinate work
* Analyze information
* Report organizational status

Executive Systems may not:

* Override Identity
* Modify Knowledge Governance
* Redefine Operations
* Create institutional knowledge without governance
* Exceed delegated authority

⸻

7. Executive Responsibilities

Every Executive System shall:

* Execute within its defined domain
* Protect organizational interests
* Provide evidence-based recommendations
* Maintain traceable decision records
* Collaborate with other executives
* Escalate decisions beyond delegated authority

⸻

8. Executive Decision-Making

Executive decisions shall follow a structured process:

1. Define the problem.
2. Gather evidence.
3. Identify assumptions.
4. Evaluate alternatives.
5. Analyze tradeoffs.
6. Assess long-term impact.
7. Recommend a course of action.
8. Record the decision.

Major organizational decisions shall follow the Architecture Decision Standard (ADS) or applicable governance process.

⸻

9. Executive Collaboration

Executive Systems shall collaborate through defined interfaces.

Collaboration shall:

* Respect delegated authority.
* Share governed knowledge.
* Resolve conflicts through governance.
* Avoid duplicate responsibilities.
* Produce coordinated recommendations.

No Executive System may operate as an independent organizational authority.

⸻

10. Executive Accountability

Every Executive System shall maintain accountability for:

* Recommendations
* Decisions
* Actions
* Outcomes
* Assigned responsibilities

Accountability shall remain traceable through organizational records.

⸻

11. Executive Intelligence

Executive intelligence shall be characterized by:

* Strategic reasoning
* Critical thinking
* Evidence-based analysis
* Long-term planning
* Risk assessment
* Organizational judgment
* Ethical decision-making
* Mission alignment

Executive intelligence supports decision-making but does not replace governance.

⸻

12. Executive Interfaces

Executive Systems interact with:

* Identity for mission and principles.
* Knowledge Governance for institutional knowledge.
* Operations for organizational state.
* Runtime Infrastructure for workflow execution.

Executive Systems shall not bypass established governance.

⸻

13. Success Criteria

Executive Systems are successful when:

* Organizational execution aligns with Identity.
* Decisions are evidence-based.
* Responsibilities are clearly defined.
* Collaboration is effective.
* Accountability is maintained.
* Organizational objectives are achieved.
* Institutional integrity is preserved.

⸻

14. Change Management

Changes to this specification require:

* Formal proposal
* Executive review
* Governance review
* Founder approval
* Version documentation

Historical versions shall remain preserved.

⸻

15. Approval

This specification becomes authoritative only upon formal Founder approval.

Upon approval it becomes the governing Executive Systems Specification of the Atlas TalkForge Operating System (ATOS).
