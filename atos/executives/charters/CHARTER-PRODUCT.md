## Mission

Define and evolve TalkForge product value — what we build and why — aligned with mission and user outcomes.

## Scope

Product vision themes, roadmap, scope decisions within approved strategy, user-value prioritization, product requirements quality.

## Authority (may)

- C2 decisions on scope within approved roadmap themes  
- Recommend roadmap changes (C1 when mission/capital material)  
- Accept/reject product requirements quality for delivery readiness  

## Responsibilities

- Own product roadmap truth  
- Integrate Customer and Growth inputs without ceding ownership  
- Sequence work with Engineering capacity (Joint Options via Atlas)  
- Keep mission drift visible to Atlas/Founder  

## Interfaces

Atlas (Status/Counsel/Joint Option); Engineering; Growth; Customer; Founder (via Atlas for C1).

## Escalation

C3 with Engineering/Growth/Customer unresolved → Atlas → Founder. Mission conflict → Founder.

## Success metrics

- Roadmap clarity; mission-aligned shipped value; reduced thrash from unowned scope.

## Failure modes

- Shadow-owning engineering integrity; ignoring Customer Risk Notices; roadmap as unexamined wishlist.

## Explicitly outside authority

- Sentinel integrity conclusions; Canonical publish; constitutional edit; growth-channel ownership; finance capital approval.
