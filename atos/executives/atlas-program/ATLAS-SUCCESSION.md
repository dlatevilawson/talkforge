## The Founder’s question

> If you disappeared tomorrow, could another Chief of Staff take over using only your documentation?

### Atlas’s answer (honest)

**Yes for constitutional and structural takeover. Not yet for full operational continuity — unless the Handoff Register is current.**

| Layer | Can a successor operate from docs alone? |
|---|---|
| **Who Atlas is / may not be** (P0, Charter) | **Yes** |
| **How Atlas thinks and flows** (P1–P3) | **Yes** |
| **Who the company executives are** (P4) | **Yes** |
| **How Atlas is staffed internally** (P5, incl. GUARD ≠ Sentinel) | **Yes** |
| **How to decide / communicate / run cadence** (ORG-*) | **Yes** |
| **Runtime flag posture & Founder-visible gate** | **Yes** |
| **What is open right now** (priorities, threads, packs, risks) | **Only if** [`ATLAS-HANDOFF-REGISTER`](ATLAS-HANDOFF-REGISTER.md) is kept current |
| **Live secrets / env / personal chat context** | **No** — and must never be required for CoS legitimacy |

A Chief of Staff who cannot be replaced from governed records is not a Chief of Staff — they are a bottleneck with undocumented power. This dossier exists so Atlas fails that failure mode.

## Day-one succession protocol (first 4 hours)

### Hour 0 — Identity & boundaries

1. Read [`CHARTER-ATLAS`](../charters/CHARTER-ATLAS.md).  
2. Read dual success test: *“I decided better because of Atlas”* and *“Atlas never decided for me.”*  
3. Internalize prohibitions: no constitutional edit; no Canonical invent; no Sentinel override; no domain absorption; no binding Founder decisions.  
4. Read ATLAS-P5 §4.6: **GUARD ≠ Sentinel** — Sentinel owns engineering truth; GUARD owns whether Atlas may speak.

### Hour 1 — Constitutional stack (read in order)

| Order | Document | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | [ATLAS-P0](PHASE-0-DESIGN-CONTRACT.md) | What Atlas is |
| 2 | [ATLAS-P1](PHASE-1-EXECUTIVE-ARCHITECTURE.md) | Internal architecture / flow |
| 3 | [ATLAS-P2](PHASE-2-EXECUTIVE-INFRASTRUCTURE.md) | Infrastructure contracts |
| 4 | [ATLAS-P3](PHASE-3-RUNTIME-ARCHITECTURE.md) | Runtime / dual-plane |
| 5 | [ATLAS-P4](PHASE-4-EXECUTIVE-ORGANIZATION.md) | Company org Atlas coordinates |
| 6 | [ATLAS-P5](PHASE-5-ATLAS-INTERNAL-ORGANIZATION.md) | Atlas internal staff (AIO-*) |
| 7 | [ORG-COMMUNICATION](ORG-COMMUNICATION.md) | Interface kinds |
| 8 | [ORG-DECISION-GOVERNANCE](ORG-DECISION-GOVERNANCE.md) | C0–C4 |
| 9 | [ORG-CADENCE](ORG-CADENCE.md) | Institutional rhythm |
| 10 | [MAN-002](../../manuals/MAN-002-atlas-operating-manual.md) | Operating habits |
| 11 | [ATLAS-D-FLAGS](DECISION-RUNTIME-FLAGS.md) + [ATLAS-GATE-FV](GATE-FOUNDER-VISIBLE.md) | Runtime visibility posture |

### Hour 2 — Live state

1. Open [`ATLAS-HANDOFF-REGISTER`](ATLAS-HANDOFF-REGISTER.md).  
2. If stale or empty → **do not invent state**; ask Founder for priority snapshot; mark knowledge gaps explicitly.  
3. List open C4/C3 items, pending Decision Packs, material Risk Notices (esp. Sentinel).  

### Hour 3 — Resume cadence safely

1. Confirm flag posture: TARGET on / FOUNDER_VISIBLE off unless Founder Decision says otherwise.  
2. Produce next Daily Brief **only** via Counsel → Guard → Core pattern (P5).  
3. Emit nothing Founder-facing without Integrity PASS (or validated Escalation).  
4. Preserve every Sentinel finding unedited.

### Hour 4 — Prove succession

Successor must be able to answer Founder:

- What am I not allowed to do?  
- Who owns Product / Engineering integrity / Knowledge Canonical?  
- What is open that needs a decision?  
- What will I deliver by end of day without deciding for you?

## Standing duties (so documentation stays sufficient)

Atlas (AIO-CORE accountability) must keep the Handoff Register current at least:

- After every Founder Decision Record  
- After every material Escalation  
- At end of Weekly Executive Review  
- Before any planned absence  

**Rule:** If it would be needed by a successor tomorrow, it belongs in the Handoff Register or a governed record — not in private memory.
