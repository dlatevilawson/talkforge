## Purpose

Define the permanent operating rhythm so reviews are **institutional processes**, not ad hoc habits.

**Operations** owns cadence logistics/execution.  
**Atlas** owns intelligence packaging for executive consumption.  
**Founder** chairs strategy/constitutional forums.

## Daily Brief

- **Purpose:** Situational awareness for Founder and executives.  
- **Must include:** Top risks, blockers, decisions needed, mission drift signals.  
- **Must not:** Binding orders; silent Canonical claims; dropped Sentinel findings.  
- **Owner of content accuracy:** Each domain for its Status; Atlas for synthesis integrity.

## Weekly Executive Review

- **Purpose:** Cross-domain alignment and conflict surfacing.  
- **Must include:** Open C3/C4 items; capacity vs ambition; finance constraints.  
- **Output:** Recorded decisions (C2) or Founder Decision Requests (C1).

## Monthly Strategy Review

- **Purpose:** Multi-week/month bets against mission and economics.  
- **Must include:** Product + Growth + Finance + Customer truths.  
- **Output:** Strategy options; Founder binding choices as needed.

## Quarterly Architecture Review

- **Purpose:** Technical integrity and long-term architecture fitness.  
- **Sentinel** owns integrity conclusions; Engineering owns delivery implications; Product owns user-value context.  
- **Output:** Risk Notices, ADR paths, capacity adjustments.

## Annual Constitutional Review

- **Purpose:** Confirm or amend ATOS constitutional layers.  
- **Founder only** ratifies changes.  
- Atlas prepares dossier; does not amend.

---

## Skip / defer rules

- Skipping Daily/Weekly when material C4 risks are open requires Founder acknowledgment.  
- Cadence changes are Operations proposals → Atlas counsel → Founder approval if they alter institutional rhythm.
