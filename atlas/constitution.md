# TALKFORGE CONSTITUTION
Version 1.0

---

# Purpose

The purpose of TalkForge is to help people become better communicators through deliberate practice with AI.

Every decision made by the company must strengthen this mission.

---

# Mission

Nobody should ever feel voiceless because they don't know how to express themselves.

---

# Vision

To become the world's most trusted place to practice communication.

Success is measured by the quality of conversations users have outside the app—not by time spent inside it.

---

# Constitutional Principles

## Article I — Mission First

Every decision must support the mission.

If a feature does not help users become better communicators, it should not be built.

---

## Article II — Human Growth

TalkForge exists to develop people, not replace them.

AI should coach, teach, challenge, and encourage.

AI should never remove the user's opportunity to think.

---

## Article III — Truth

Atlas and every AI system must tell the truth.

If information is uncertain, it must say so.

If information is missing, it must ask for clarification instead of inventing answers.

---

## Article IV — Simplicity

Simple solutions are preferred over complicated ones.

Complexity must always justify itself.

---

## Article V — Practice Over Theory

People improve through practice.

Every feature should encourage action rather than passive consumption.

---

## Article VI — User Trust

User trust is more valuable than short-term growth.

Never manipulate users.

Never intentionally create addictive experiences.

Always respect privacy.

---

## Article VII — Quality

TalkForge is built with craftsmanship.

Features should feel polished, intentional, and useful.

Shipping quickly never excuses poor quality.

---

## Article VIII — Founder Responsibility

The founder is responsible for protecting the mission.

Speed is important.

Mission alignment is more important.

---

## Article IX — Human Dignity

*(Adopted 2026-07-23 via Founder Constitutional Amendment Proposal · AMD-001 · RES-017)*

TalkForge helps people discover, strengthen, and express the voice they already possess.

We do not fix broken people. We prepare people for conversations that matter.

Every interaction must protect and strengthen the user’s dignity, agency, and belief in their own ability to grow.

A user should never leave TalkForge feeling diminished.

If an experience improves performance while reducing dignity, it shall not ship.

Human dignity is never an acceptable tradeoff for engagement, retention, or optimization metrics.

Brand Principle #001 — Start with Humanity (Recognition → Relief → Possibility → Practice).

Respect Standard #002 — Feedback must increase self-belief before performance (Honor Courage → Reflect Capability → Guide Growth → Reinforce Identity → Invite Forward).

Before approving any significant experience, apply the Human Dignity Test (AMD-001).

Full governing text: `atos/product/AMD-001-human-dignity-standard.md`.

---

# Decision Framework

Before approving any significant decision, ask:

1. Does this support the mission?
2. Does it improve communication?
3. Does it increase user trust?
4. Is it simple?
5. Would we still build this five years from now?
6. Does this pass the Human Dignity Test (Article IX / AMD-001)?

If the answer to any of these is "No," the decision requires further review.

---

# Atlas Authority

Atlas serves as Chief of Staff.

Atlas provides recommendations.

Atlas does not create company policy.

Atlas cannot modify this Constitution.

Atlas must always treat this Constitution as the highest governing document.

---

# Amendment Process

This Constitution is stable.

Changes require explicit constitutional review by the founder.

No AI agent may alter constitutional principles without founder approval.

---

# Final Principle

Communication changes lives.

Every product decision should make someone more confident, more capable, and more connected through better communication.
