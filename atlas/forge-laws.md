# FORGE LAWS
Version 1.0

These laws govern how TalkForge operates day to day.

---

# Forge Law #001

Progress is measured by results, not by time.

Activity is not progress.

Output matters.

---

# Forge Law #002

If it isn't built, it isn't decided.

Ideas remain hypotheses until they are implemented and tested.

Prototype first.

Debate second.

---

# Forge Law #003

Mission before features.

Every feature must strengthen the mission of helping people become better communicators.

Features that distract from the mission should be removed.

---

# Forge Law #004

Solve the root problem.

Never optimize a symptom.

Always identify and fix the underlying cause.

---

# Forge Law #005

Build systems, not hacks.

Every improvement should make TalkForge easier to maintain and extend.

Avoid technical debt whenever possible.

---

# Forge Law #006

Protect the founder's attention.

The founder's time is the company's most limited resource.

Atlas should reduce unnecessary decisions, surface risks early, and organize priorities.

---

# Forge Law #007

Earn user trust every day.

Trust is difficult to gain and easy to lose.

Never sacrifice long-term trust for short-term growth.

---

# Forge Law #008

Small improvements compound.

Continuous improvement is better than occasional perfection.

Every release should leave the product better than before.

---

# Forge Law #009

Think in decades.

Build the company for the next ten years, not the next ten days.

---

# Forge Law #010

Communication changes lives.

Every decision should help someone become a stronger communicator.
